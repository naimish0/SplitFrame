No SplitFrame account or cloud service is required.

Selected media is edited on your device and is not uploaded to a SplitFrame-operated server. Google Mobile Ads uses network data as described below.

1. Summary

  • SplitFrame does not provide account creation or sign-in.
  • You choose the photos, videos, and optional audio that the app may access.
  • Editing, preview generation, and export processing happen on your device.
  • Your selected media is not provided to Google for advertising.
  • Google Mobile Ads may collect and share device and usage data when ads are requested.
  • You control when an export is saved or shared with another app.

3. Media processing, exports, and sharing

SplitFrame uses Android's system picker so you can grant access to specific media instead of your entire library. On devices where the modern picker is unavailable, Android may use its document picker fallback. The app may keep a system-granted reference to selected media so a draft, batch, or video project can continue later.

Image and video processing is performed locally. Video export uses an app-private temporary file. SplitFrame attempts to remove temporary output after the export and maintains a recovery record so an interrupted, app-owned export can be reconciled on the next startup.

When you tap Share, Android's share sheet gives the chosen exported file to the app or recipient you select. Their privacy practices apply after the transfer.

Photo metadata

Resize exports remove metadata by default. If you explicitly choose to preserve details, SplitFrame may copy a limited set of EXIF fields into the exported image, including date, camera, copyright, exposure, and location fields that exist in the source. Review that setting before sharing an export.

4. Advertising and consent

SplitFrame uses Google Mobile Ads to provide banner, native, interstitial, and app-open advertisements. According to Google's current Android SDK disclosure, the SDK automatically collects and shares IP address, user product interactions, diagnostic information, and device or account identifiers for advertising, analytics, and fraud prevention. Google states that this information is encrypted in transit using TLS.

Where required, SplitFrame uses Google's User Messaging Platform to request or manage advertising choices before enabling ad requests. When Google indicates that privacy options are required, the app displays a Manage advertising privacy action on its in-app Privacy screen. Your choice may result in personalized, non-personalized, or limited ads; it may not remove all advertising.

Google's own terms and retention practices apply to information it processes:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • How Google uses information from apps that use its services
  • Google Mobile Ads SDK data disclosure

5. Permissions and system access

  • Selected media access: the system photo or document picker grants access to items you choose.
  • Legacy storage: Android 7 through 9 may request storage permission only when saving an export to shared media storage.
  • Network and advertising services: Google Mobile Ads adds internet, network-state, advertising ID, and applicable Android Privacy Sandbox permissions.
  • Foreground processing and wake lock: Android may keep a long video export running and display the required foreground-service status.

SplitFrame does not request broad photo or video library permission on supported modern Android versions.

6. Storage, retention, and deletion

  • Private drafts, preferences, presets, favorites, recent references, and project records remain on your device until replaced, deleted where supported, app storage is cleared, or the app is uninstalled.
  • Video projects can be deleted from the app. Clearing app storage removes all private SplitFrame records and cache files.
  • App backup and device-transfer backup are disabled for SplitFrame's private data.
  • Exports in your gallery survive app-data clearing and uninstallation. Delete them using your gallery, file manager, or Android settings.
  • Google controls retention of advertising information under Google's policies.

7. Your choices and rights

  • Choose which media to provide, or cancel the system picker.
  • Remove or reset drafts and video projects using available in-app controls.
  • Clear SplitFrame storage or uninstall the app to remove its private local data.
  • Delete exported files separately from shared media storage.
  • Use Manage advertising privacy when available and manage the advertising ID in Android settings.
  • Contact the publisher regarding applicable privacy rights or questions.

Because SplitFrame has no user account or publisher-operated data server, the publisher generally cannot identify or remotely delete private data stored only on your device. Google provides its own privacy tools for data processed through its services.

8. Security and international processing

SplitFrame relies on Android's application sandbox, system picker, scoped media storage, and permission controls. Private app backup is disabled. Google states that Mobile Ads data is encrypted in transit. No storage or transmission method is completely secure, so absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

Google may process advertising information in countries other than your own under its privacy policy and applicable transfer safeguards.

9. Children

SplitFrame is not directed to children under 13, and the publisher does not knowingly collect personal information directly from children. If you believe a child has provided information in connection with the app, contact the publisher.

10. Changes to this policy

This policy may change when SplitFrame's features, SDKs, or legal obligations change. The updated version will be posted at the same public location with a revised effective or verification date. Material changes should also be reflected in the app's in-app notice and Google Play Data safety declarations.
