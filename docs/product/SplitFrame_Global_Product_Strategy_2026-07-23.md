Evidence and method

This is a decision report, not a forecast of guaranteed installs. No feature, localization, price,
or roadmap can guarantee that SplitFrame will become the most-installed app in its category.

Store facts are current Google Play header values observed on the research date. Google Play exposes
install bands, not exact installs; this report never converts bands into invented totals. Ratings
and review counts can vary by country, account, device, and time.

Publisher claim means the app’s Play copy, site, or policy makes the claim; it was not independently
code-audited.

Reviewer report means a theme appeared in a manual sample of currently featured Play reviews. This
was not a complete review export or statistically representative sentiment study.

Analyst inference is explicitly marked. It combines the product brief, competitive evidence,
regional platform context, and implementation feasibility.

The baseline locale for every competitor was en-US unless a row says otherwise. Cross-checks
included en-CA, en-GB, en-AU, en-IN, en-ZA, fr-FR, pl-PL, and en-SG. Localized listing text is not
proof of full in-app localization.

A current, comparable public time series was not found for competitor installs. Therefore, this
report does not label an app “fastest-growing” merely because it has a large install band or many
reviews.

Public Play listings do not provide a stable, device-comparable installed-size measure. App-size
comparisons are therefore omitted rather than guessed.

Search demand is inferred cautiously from repeated high-intent wording in successful Play
titles/descriptions and review use cases. It is not presented as keyword-volume data.

1. Executive summary

Decision

SplitFrame should launch as a focused, private, on-device utility suite, not as a miniature CapCut,
Canva, or Picsart. Its most defensible wedge is:

Fast everyday collage, resize, compression, and sequential video joining—without an account, media
upload, watermark, or professional timeline.

The market is already validated by very large install bands across each component workflow: collage
apps such as Collage Maker and PicCollage, resize utilities such as Photo & Picture Resizer, and
accessible video editors such as InShot and YouCut. That validates demand, but it does not prove
that combining all three automatically creates acquisition.

What to do

Treat output correctness as the launch gate. Featured reviews across Momentic, PhotoGrid, Scoompa,
PicPlayPost, VN, and Adobe Express repeatedly mention blank, cropped, failed, frozen, or lost
output. SplitFrame must make “preview equals export” and recoverable output a measurable promise.

Build target-KB/MB compression and batch resize/compression next. These are explicit headline
capabilities in Photo & Picture Resizer, Puma, QReduce, and Compress Image Size in KB & MB. They
solve a clearer repeat utility than adding more templates to an already large set of 110.

Improve discovery, saved work, and first-use proof. Rename weak templates, add visual search facets,
provide one optional sample project, and give photo/resize work the same recoverable history concept
as video.

Localize the listing before translating the whole app. Start with Spanish and Brazilian Portuguese,
then Indonesian and a Hindi listing experiment. Use country-specific screenshots and preset order.
Google Play supports localized and custom store listings; custom listings can vary name, icon,
descriptions, and assets for selected countries or acquisition routes, but they do not
auto-translate (Google Play Help).

Offer a transparent one-time “Remove ads” purchase after the free launch stabilizes. Keep core
export free and watermark-free. Do not introduce a subscription until SplitFrame offers recurring
content or service value.

What not to do

Do not claim “video collage” while video output is sequential only. Do not rush into generative AI,
cloud accounts, a creative-asset subscription, a full multitrack timeline, or a bundled music
catalog. Those moves weaken the focused offline promise, raise ongoing cost and policy exposure, and
compete directly with apps operating at 100M+ to 1B+ install bands.

Regional thesis

Broad-demand hypotheses with the strongest evidence: reliable export, simple flows, no surprise
watermark, useful ratios, target file size, batch utilities, recoverable projects, and restrained
ads.

Higher-Android-share/lower-resource market hypotheses: low-memory behavior, predictable output size,
720p/1080p fallbacks, resumable jobs, WhatsApp/vertical presets, local-language listings, and
purchasing-power-aware prices.

Mature-market hypotheses: polished typography/layouts, high-resolution fidelity, tablet quality,
metadata control, native-quality localization, privacy clarity, and an ad-free purchase.

Market-specific—not global-core—work: LINE/Kakao share affordances, RTL-first Arabic presentation,
Ramadan/Eid merchandising, regional marketplace presets, and destination ordering. Implement these
as configuration/localization, not separate editing engines.

Ranking cautions

Play Data Safety is a developer declaration, not an independent privacy audit. Several competitors
show discrepancies between a short store declaration and a longer publisher policy. SplitFrame can
differentiate by keeping its privacy page, policy, SDK inventory, and Data Safety form exactly
aligned.

“No offline claim” does not prove an app cannot work offline. The only especially clear adjacent
publisher claim found was VN’s online/offline statement.

Review totals are not comparable with scientific precision across locales. They are used as scale
indicators, not market-share estimates.

3. Regional competitor differences

The percentages below are June 2026 mobile web operating-system usage shares, not Play installs,
active devices, or revenue. They are included only as directional device-context evidence from
StatCounter. Market priorities also use DataReportal’s Digital 2026 country reports, which generally
describe late-2025 observations and often count social-media user identities rather than unique
people.

No authoritative country-by-country Play category ranking or competitor revenue/ad-tolerance panel
was available in the evidence gathered. Therefore, this report does not invent regional “leaders” or
willingness-to-pay values. Presence checks showed PicCollage in Canada; InShot, VN, Picsart, and
Scoompa in the UK; PhotoGrid, InShot, Canva, Momentic, and QReduce in India; and selected
cross-checks in Australia, South Africa, France, Poland, and Singapore. Presence is not leadership.

implications below are analyst hypotheses to validate, not competitor-share findings. Privacy
statements distinguish legal/platform requirements—such as certified consent in relevant European ad
contexts—from unmeasured consumer preference.

Regional conclusions

Universal preset architecture: maintain one canonical ratio library—9:16, 4:5, 1:1, 16:9, 3:4/4:3,
2:3, original, custom—and reorder destination labels by locale. Do not duplicate the same dimensions
under dozens of hard-coded platform names.

High-growth-market core: target file size, batch processing, visible output path, job recovery,
sensible input caps, 720p/1080p fallbacks, low-memory bitmap handling, and smaller downloads.

Mature-market core: export fidelity, metadata choice, print units, tablet polish, native-quality
language, accessibility, and a clean ad-free option.

Market configuration only: LINE/Kakao labels, Ramadan/Eid creative, country marketplaces, and
specific share-target ordering. Validate through custom-listing conversion before making global
navigation changes.

Regional social/platform use cases are product hypotheses grounded in current country digital
context, not claims of precise SplitFrame search volume. Validate them with localized listing
experiments and first-party Play acquisition data.

6. SplitFrame’s strongest existing advantages

A coherent offline/no-account promise. Core media work is local, basic use requires no account,
backup is disabled, and media is not intentionally uploaded. Large adjacent suites often center
cloud, accounts, content, or AI.

Three useful jobs without a professional timeline. Collage, image resize/convert, and sequential
joining share a “choose → adjust → export” mental model.

A genuinely deep collage foundation. 110 runtime layouts, 1–15 slots, local recommendations, search,
filters, favorites, recents, multiple shapes/backgrounds/borders, text, before/after, and undo/redo
are already competitive for a focused 1.0.

No intentional watermark and no core export paywall. This aligns with a recurring value signal in
YouCut/VN reviews and avoids export-time betrayal.

Unusually thoughtful video job ownership. Saved projects, rename/duplicate/delete, lifecycle
recovery, WorkManager export, progress/cancel, and correct notification routing are stronger than a
bare “join clips” utility.

Modern Android platform choices. Compose, Room, MediaStore, Photo Picker, WorkManager, and Media3
Transformer are appropriate foundations. Media3 supports multi-asset sequences and compositions (
multi-asset editing, compositions).

Honest technical language. Deterministic layout scoring is not called AI; conventional scaling is
not called enhancement; sequential joining is not called collage.

A meaningful existing test base. Approximately 249 JVM and 44 Android tests reduce ordinary
regression risk. The remaining issue is media-corpus breadth and device/output validation, not an
absence of engineering discipline.

The strongest differentiation is the combination of focus, local processing, honest claims, and
recoverable output. None is unique alone; together they form a credible trust position.

Recommended model

Free core: collage, resize/convert, sequential merge, local export, recovery, and no watermark
remain usable without payment.

Ads: retain restrained banner/native inventory and occasional post-success interstitials, with
explicit frequency caps.

One-time non-consumable “Remove ads”: launch after stability and ad-baseline measurement. Google
Play supports one-time non-consumable products and regional pricing (one-time products, regional

No subscription yet: there is no recurring service/content benefit that justifies one. Do not put
basic export, recovery, or watermark removal behind recurring payment.

Price test, not a universal price claim

Use a US control around
US$4.99 one time, then, where the current Console supports it, test roughly -20% and +40% variants under Google Play’s current one-time-product pricing guidance (Google Play Help). Apply purchasing-power-aware regional pricing rather than mechanical currency conversion. This is a proposed experiment, not evidence that US$
4.99 is optimal. Measure conversion, refunds, ad revenue displaced, 30-day retention, and net
revenue per active user.

Ad rules

No ads during picker, editing, permission/consent, export, error recovery, or sharing.

No app-open ad on first session. Use it only at genuine app-open/foreground moments with a loading
context and a long cooldown. Google’s guidance says app-open ads are for loading/opening moments and
notes that apps opened more often than every four hours are a strong fit; it does not justify
showing one at every resume (AdMob app-open guidance).

Interstitial only after a verified successful workflow or another natural break; never at app
load/exit or after every action. AdMob explicitly prohibits repeated/interfering placement
patterns (interstitial guidance).

Reserve fixed banner space to prevent layout jumps and accidental taps (banner guidance).

Use frequency caps (AdMob) and a certified consent platform where required.

Make native ads visually distinct and labeled. “No media upload” must not be shortened to “no data
collection” while ad/consent SDKs communicate.

Telemetry boundary

Start with Android vitals, Play acquisition/listing performance, billing reports, and reviews.
Google currently defines overall bad-behavior thresholds of 1.09% for user-perceived crash rate and
0.47% for user-perceived ANR rate; crossing thresholds can affect Play visibility (Android vitals).
SplitFrame’s internal release goals should be stricter—for example, crash-free users ≥99.8% and ANR
below 0.3%—without pretending those targets are guaranteed.

Only add a third-party analytics/crash SDK if those sources cannot answer a concrete decision. If
added, disclose it, update Data Safety/consent, and collect coarse workflow outcome/error
information only—never filenames, URIs, media pixels, captions, contacts, or selected-media
metadata.

Make export correctness a product feature: preflight codec/storage/workload, validate output,

Unify recent work across collage, resize, and video: resume, duplicate, re-export, delete/undo, and

Turn the existing 110 layouts into a discoverable product: curated names/tags, visual examples,

Create locale-aware presets and quick entry: one ratio registry, regional destination ordering,
