Privacy Policy for SplitFrame

Effective date: July 18, 2026
Developer: Naimish Gupta

SplitFrame creates photo collages, resizes images, and merges videos. This Privacy Policy explains what information SplitFrame accesses, how it is used, when it is shared, and the choices available to users.

Summary

  • SplitFrame does not require an account.
  • Photos and videos selected for editing are processed on the device and are not uploaded to a SplitFrame-operated server.
  • SplitFrame uses Google AdMob. When ads are requested, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may automatically collect and share certain device and usage data for advertising, analytics, and fraud prevention.
  • Users control when they select, export, or share media.

Media and Other Data Processed on the Device

When a user chooses media through Android's system picker, SplitFrame accesses only the selected photos or videos. The app uses that media to display previews, apply requested edits, create collages, resize images, merge videos, and produce exports.

SplitFrame may store the following app data locally on the device:

  • references to selected media, such as Android content URIs;
  • media metadata needed for editing, such as dimensions, duration, file type, file size, frame rate, audio availability, rotation, and HDR status;
  • editing choices, such as template, crop, zoom, position, trim, background, resolution, and output settings;
  • favorite templates, export history, saved-output references, and background-export status or error information; and
  • temporary image or video files created while processing an export.

This information is used only to provide and maintain SplitFrame's editing and export features. SplitFrame does not use selected photos or videos for advertising and does not send their contents to Google AdMob.

Exports and User-Initiated Sharing

When a user exports a result, SplitFrame saves it to Android media storage, normally under Pictures/SplitFrame or Movies/SplitFrame. An exported file remains there until the user deletes it using a gallery or file-management app, or Android settings.

When a user taps Share, Android's system share sheet sends the selected export only to the app or person the user chooses. The recipient's privacy practices apply after the user shares the file.

Advertising Data

SplitFrame uses the Google Mobile Ads SDK to display banner, native, interstitial, and app-open advertisements. When the SDK is allowed to request ads, Google's current SDK disclosure says it automatically collects and shares the following data:

  • IP address, which may be used to estimate approximate location;
  • app interactions, including app launches, taps, and ad or video views;
  • diagnostics and app-performance information, including launch time, hang rate, and energy usage; and
  • device or account identifiers, including the Android advertising ID, app set ID, and, when applicable, other identifiers related to accounts signed in on the device.

Google uses this data for advertising or marketing, analytics, and fraud prevention, security, and compliance. Google states that this data is encrypted in transit using Transport Layer Security (TLS). Google may process information in countries other than the user's country under its own terms and policies.

Advertising and Privacy Choices

Where required, SplitFrame presents Google's consent message before requesting ads. The app also provides access to Google's privacy-options form when Google indicates that privacy options are required.

Users can reset or delete the Android advertising ID in Android settings, typically under Privacy > Ads. Depending on the device and region, users may also be able to limit ad personalization through Android or Google account settings. These choices may result in non-personalized or limited ads rather than removing all advertising.

Permissions and System Access

Depending on the Android version and the action selected, SplitFrame may use:

  • Android's system photo picker to access only media the user selects;
  • storage access on older Android versions to save requested exports;
  • foreground processing so a video export can continue while the app is not in the foreground.

SplitFrame does not request broad access to all photos and videos on supported modern Android versions.

Storage, Backup, Retention, and Deletion

Selected media remains in the user's own media library. The current video project can be reset in the app. Preferences, favorites, and export-history records remain until app storage is cleared or the app is uninstalled. Exported files remain in shared media storage until the user deletes them separately. Temporary video-export files are deleted after the export attempt finishes; Android may also clear cache files automatically.

SplitFrame disables Android backup and device transfer for its private app data. Clearing SplitFrame's app storage or uninstalling the app removes its local database and cached files, but does not delete exported files from the gallery.

Advertising data is retained by Google according to Google's policies and the user's Google and Android privacy settings. SplitFrame does not control Google's retention periods. Users may use the privacy controls described above and Google's privacy tools to manage that data.

Security

Media editing is performed locally on the device. SplitFrame relies on Android's app sandbox, system media picker, scoped media storage, and permission controls to limit access. Advertising data sent by the Google Mobile Ads SDK is encrypted in transit as described by Google. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, so absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

Data Sales and Other Sharing

SplitFrame does not sell selected photos or videos. Information is shared only in the following circumstances:

  • with Google through the Google Mobile Ads SDK as described above;
  • with a recipient selected by the user through Android's share sheet;
  • when required to comply with applicable law or a valid legal process.

Accounts and Children

SplitFrame does not provide account creation or sign-in, so there is no SplitFrame account to delete.

SplitFrame is not directed to children under 13. If the publisher learns that information from a child has been handled in a way inconsistent with this policy, the publisher will review the matter after being contacted through the method below.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when SplitFrame's features, SDKs, or legal obligations change. The revised policy will be published at the same public URL with a new effective date.
